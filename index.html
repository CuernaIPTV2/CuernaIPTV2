What is IPTV?

IPTV stands for Internet Protocol Television. Instead of using traditional cable or satellite signals, IPTV delivers television programs and videos through your internet connection. This means you can watch live channels, movies, and series on demand using the same technology that powers websites and streaming services. If you can browse the web, you can use IPTV — it’s TV made simple.

To access IPTV, you use applications such as TiviMate, IPTV Smarters, Sparkle TV, M‑Tivi, or IBO. These apps connect you to different providers, just like a web browser connects you to websites.

Think of it like this: you need an Internet Provider (ISP) such as Telmex, Totalplay, Megacable, or Infinitum; a web browser like Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge; and a device such as a computer, tablet, or smart TV. IPTV works the same way — your ISP provides the connection, your IPTV app acts like the browser, and your device is where you watch. Most Cuernavaca IPTV customers currently use TiviMate or M‑Tivi.

⚠️ Important Requirement: A bi‑directional internet connection of at least 30 Mbps is required at the location where you want to use the service. This ensures smooth playback, especially for live channels and HD content.
